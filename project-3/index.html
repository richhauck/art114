Project 3

Project Description:

You will create a responsive website for the Fenetre Gallery, a (defunct) art gallery at HACC’s midtown campus. The purpose of the site will be to promote exhibits, special events, programs, and communicate how to visit the gallery.

While you will be provided with a logo and some content, you are encouraged to create your own mock materials. For example, you could create an exhibit using another artist’s work (provided you have their permission), your own artwork (provided it is not part of your graphic design portfolio), or of artwork found on Creative Commons (https://ccsearch.creativecommons.org). You are welcome to modify or replace the logo, but you will be responsible for defining branding with a minimum of two fonts and five colors.
ain at least one image gallery with a slideshow and/or lightbox (the gallery could be for the current exhibit, permanent collection, etc.). This gallery will contain a minimum of six images of both vertical and horizontal orientation. This could be a page presenting the current exhibit, “permanent collection,” etc. The site should also utilize some sort of custom CSS or JavaScript animation.

For research, visit art gallery and museum websites and examine their site structure. See what messages are on the homepage. Recognize that every piece of art should be accompanied with a title and artist name.

Development Requirements:

  1. Website contains a minimum of one image gallery with a minimum of six images. The gallery should contain both portrait and landscape-oriented images. The gallery can be presented as a responsive slideshow and/or lightbox, but artist attribution must be present for each work.
  2. CSS animations and/or transitions should be incorporated into the design.
  3. Website should contain proper page titles for SEO.
  4. Everything should function properly on student’s hosting at student’s website URL.
  5. A Google Maps map should be embedded.

Process:

  1. Sitemap. You will draw up a sitemap outlining the structure of the website.
  2. Wireframes. You will develop a wireframe outlining all the major pages throughout the site.
  3. Branding. You will choose a minimum of two fonts and five colors for use with the site.
  4. Design Comps. Upon wireframe approval, you will create interactive designs in Adobe XD based on the wireframe.
  5. Design Critique. You will take part in an in-class design critique where you will provide constructive criticism to your classmates on their designs, as well as receive feedback on yours.
  6. Revisions. You will revise your design to address improvements uncovered during the critique stage.
  7. Development. You will proceed to build the website. Upon completion, it will be uploaded to your website.
  8. Final Critique. You will participate in a final group critique where we review each of the projects.
