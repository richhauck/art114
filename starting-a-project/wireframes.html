Wireframes

Wireframes are an intermediary step between the sitemap and the visual design. During this stage, the information architecture of the page is being defined. The fidelity of the wireframe can be as simple as a sketch with content boxes, or be nearly as polished as a final design. Depending on the organization and scale of the project, this stage might be handled by a completely different person than the visual designer.

Tips when making wireframes:

  • Develop desktop and mobile wireframes at the same time. Doing so will help you identify problem points between the different sizes and how to solve them.
  • User color sparingly, if at all. Even small splashes of colors will draw attention in a wireframe, and the emphasis here is to define the information hierarchy. Instead, use variations of gray and black, and consider converting colored assets (logos, etc.) in black and white.
  • Stick to a plain font. Choosing fonts will become the responsibility of the visual designer.
  • Don't place photographs. Images can change depending upon the page, and a wireframe should define the interface. Instead, use gray boxes, as a median value will best represent the range a photograph may display.
